# Full Use Case Page

## Tabletop Collection Manager

### Turning a personal hobby problem into a focused product strategy

![Placeholder hero image showing mobile screens for a tabletop game collection app](./images/tabletop-collection-manager-hero-placeholder.png)

Tabletop game collections can grow quickly, but the experience of choosing what to play often gets harder over time. Players may own dozens of games, but still struggle to answer simple questions:

**What do I own? Where is it organized? What have we played? What should we play next?**

The **Tabletop Collection Manager** is a mobile-first responsive web app concept designed to help tabletop players organize their collections, track play history, understand what they actually use, and choose the right game for the right group.

---

## The Challenge

Many existing tools focus on cataloging games, public ratings, or community features. This concept starts from a more personal and practical need: helping a player or household make better use of the games they already own.

The product needed to stay focused for an MVP, avoiding early scope creep into social networking, marketplaces, native apps, or full community features.

![Placeholder image showing the problem: too many games, hard to choose what to play](./images/tabletop-collection-manager-problem-placeholder.png)

- Separating MVP scope from future enhancements
- Mapping core workflows
- Designing feature concepts for shelves, play logging, player profiles, and recommendations
- Creating validation questions and prototype testing tasks

## Product Approach

The core experience is built around a simple workflow:

**Add → Organize → Find → Play → Learn**

Users can add games manually, import collection data, organize games into manual or smart shelves, find something to play using filters, log play sessions, and review collection-health insights over time.

A key design principle is that recommendations should be **explainable and user-controlled**, not a black-box algorithm. For example, instead of simply suggesting a game, the app explains why it fits:

> Works for 4 players, takes under 45 minutes, is easy to teach, and has been rated positively by this group before.

![Placeholder image showing the Add → Organize → Find → Play → Learn workflow](./images/tabletop-collection-manager-workflow-placeholder.png)

## Key MVP Features

The MVP focuses on practical collection value:

- Private personal collection management
- BoardGameGeek-assisted game metadata
- Manual, smart, and hybrid shelves
- Filter-based “Find a Game” experience
- Quick and detailed play logging
- Reusable player profiles
- Private ratings and lightweight session reactions
- Session sharing without exposing the full collection
- Collection-health insights
- Export and deletion controls

![Placeholder image showing sample MVP feature cards or mobile UI screens](./images/tabletop-collection-manager-mvp-placeholder.png)

## Product Decisions

A major part of the work was deciding what **not** to build first.

Public reviews, social feeds, marketplaces, barcode scanning, advanced recommendations, and native mobile apps were intentionally moved out of MVP scope. This keeps the first version focused on validating whether users value collection organization, play decision support, and lightweight play tracking.
