# Product Brief

## Project name

Scott Whitlock Portfolio Site

## One-sentence summary

Build a small static personal career landing page for Scott Whitlock that helps prospective hiring staff quickly understand his Product Management positioning and take the next step.

## Primary audience

Prospective hiring staff before or after an initial interview, including:

- Recruiters
- Hiring managers
- Interview panels
- Talent acquisition staff
- AI-assisted application screening systems

## Primary goal

The site should answer three questions within the first 15 seconds:

1. What role is Scott seeking?
2. Who is Scott as a Product Manager?
3. How can someone learn more or contact him?

## Desired role positioning

Scott is seeking Product Manager roles.

Primary positioning:

> Product Manager with a design foundation, technical fluency, cross-functional leadership experience, and a practical approach to AI-enabled workflows.

Role sought line:

> Available now for Product Manager roles, remote or Cincinnati-area, where he can connect user needs, workflow design, technical teams, and practical AI adoption.

AI-readable structured positioning:

```text
Name: Scott Whitlock
Target role: Product Manager
Availability: Available now
Location preference: Remote or Cincinnati-area
Focus areas: Product Management, internal tools, workflow platforms, AI adoption, cross-functional product leadership, UX-minded discovery, technical collaboration
```

## Site type

Single-page personal landing page.

The site should feel like a modern career leavebehind, not a full case-study portfolio or resume replacement.

## Content strategy

Use the page as a curated front door. Link out to deeper sources instead of duplicating all material.

Deeper follow-up paths:

- LinkedIn for career history, roles, skills, and recommendations.
- Downloadable PDF resume for formal application review.
- Collapsed About section for deeper narrative context.
- Career Advocate chatbot for guided Q&A grounded in Scott's provided materials.

## Included in v1

- Hero section
- Contact actions
- LinkedIn link
- Downloadable PDF resume
- Short bio
- Product management strength cards
- Collapsed About section
- AI Career Advocate callout
- Learn More links
- Minimal footer
- Light and dark mode
- GitHub Actions CI/CD
- DreamHost static deployment workflow

## Excluded from v1

- Case studies
- Work samples
- Project artifact galleries
- Detailed employer timeline
- Detailed employment history
- Contact form
- Calendar scheduler
- vCard
- Blog
- Newsletter
- Backend features

## Employer history approach

Do not detail employer history on the main page for v1. The page may say:

> Full career history, roles, and recommendations are available on LinkedIn and in the downloadable resume.

Employer-specific questions can be handled by the Career Advocate chatbot, assuming it is grounded in approved materials.

## CTA priority

1. Contact Scott
2. View LinkedIn
3. Download Resume
4. Ask Scott's Career Advocate
5. Read About

The AI Career Advocate should be visually featured as a newly added capability, but it should not outrank direct contact.

## Success criteria

The site is successful if a hiring staff member can quickly:

- Understand Scott is available now for Product Manager roles.
- Understand his strengths in product discovery, cross-functional leadership, UX-minded thinking, technical fluency, practical AI adoption, and internal/content platforms.
- Contact him without friction.
- Find LinkedIn and resume quickly.
- Optionally use the Career Advocate chatbot to learn more.
